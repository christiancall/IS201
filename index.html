Ghana

A Beautiful Land of Freedom and Justice

Ghana was the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence from colonial rule.

Ghana gained independence from Great Britain in 1957.

The name “Ghana” means “warrior king” in the Soninke language.

However, the Soninke Language is not used in Ghana; English is the National Language.

Ghana is located on the west coast of Africa.

Its neighbouring countries are Côte d'Ivoire to the West, Burkina Faso to the North, Togo to the East, and the Atlantic Ocean to the South.

The currency in Ghana is Cedi. The word Cedi means Cowry Shells.

After independence, Ghana separated itself from the British West African pound, which was the currency of the British colonies in the region.
